Kevin Conroy (1955 - 2022)

Kevin Conroy was more than just a voice actor—he was the definitive voice of Batman for generations of fans. Born on November 30, 1955, in Westbury, New York, Conroy brought depth, gravitas, and humanity to the Caped Crusader, making the character his own in a way that resonated with audiences worldwide.

Conroy's journey as Batman began in 1992 with Batman: The Animated Series, a groundbreaking show that redefined superhero storytelling. His portrayal of Bruce Wayne and Batman was unparalleled, capturing the duality of the character—the brooding intensity of the Dark Knight and the charismatic charm of the billionaire playboy. His voice became synonymous with Batman, earning him a place in the hearts of fans and solidifying his legacy as one of the greatest interpreters of the character.

Over the years, Conroy lent his iconic voice to numerous projects, including Justice League, Justice League Unlimited, Batman: Mask of the Phantasm, Batman: Arkham video games, and many more. His work transcended the screen, inspiring countless fans and influencing future portrayals of Batman.

Beyond his work as Batman, Conroy was a talented stage and screen actor, with a career that spanned theater, television, and film. He was also an advocate for the LGBTQ+ community, openly sharing his experiences as a gay man and using his platform to promote acceptance and equality.

Kevin Conroy passed away on November 10, 2022, leaving behind a legacy that will endure for generations. His voice will forever echo in the halls of Gotham, reminding us why Batman is not just a hero, but a symbol of hope, justice, and resilience.

"I am vengeance. I am the night. I am Batman." — Kevin Conroy, Batman: The Animated Series
Thank you, Kevin, for giving us the Batman we needed—and the one we deserved.

Timeline

1955
  • Born on November 30 in Westbury, New York.
1970s–1980s
  • Trained at the Juilliard School, where he honed his craft as a stage actor.
  • Performed in numerous theatrical productions, including Shakespearean plays, showcasing his versatility and talent.
  • Appeared in TV shows like Another World and Dynasty, as well as films, establishing himself as a skilled screen actor.
1992
  • Landed the role of Bruce Wayne/Batman in Batman: The Animated Series. His iconic voice brought depth and complexity to the character, redefining Batman for a new generation.
1993
  • Voiced Batman in Batman: Mask of the Phantasm, the animated feature film based on Batman: The Animated Series. The film is now considered a classic and a masterpiece of superhero storytelling.
1997–1999
  • Continued to voice Batman in The New Batman Adventures, further solidifying his status as the definitive voice of the Dark Knight.
2001–2004
  • Reprised his role as Batman in Justice League and Justice League Unlimited, expanding the character's presence in the DC Animated Universe and showcasing his ability to portray Batman as both a leader and a loner.
2009–2015
  • Voiced Batman in the critically acclaimed Batman: Arkham video game series (Arkham Asylum, Arkham City, Arkham Knight). His performance brought emotional depth to the games, earning praise from fans and critics alike.
2016
  • Published Finding Batman, a heartfelt autobiographical comic as part of DC's Pride Anthology. In it, he shared his experiences as a gay man and how his identity influenced his portrayal of Batman.
2019
  • Made a live-action appearance as Bruce Wayne in the CW's Crisis on Infinite Earths crossover event, fulfilling a lifelong dream of portraying the character on screen.
2022
  • Passed away on November 10 at the age of 66, leaving behind an unparalleled legacy as the voice of Batman and an inspiration to millions.
